Thesis and Risks
Ondo is the structurally dominant play on tokenized US Treasuries. USDY's permissionless design (vs. OUSG's accredited-only access) materially expands the addressable market, and the 9-chain deployment captures yield demand wherever on-chain liquidity concentrates.

Bull: Tokenized T-bills become DeFi's reserve asset; Ondo's compliance moat and first-mover brand sustain 30%+ market share as institutional flows accelerate.

Bear: At 0.96× TVL/FDV, the market is pricing in near-zero growth premium. BlackRock BUIDL and Franklin BENJI have institutional distribution Ondo lacks. Rate cuts reduce the product yield and the thesis simultaneously. FDV/ARR of ~87× requires significant revenue growth to justify.

Thesis and Risks
Maple is the only protocol in this coverage set with meaningful, auditable revenue at a reasonable valuation multiple. The syrupUSDC flywheel — yield-bearing stablecoin with DeFi composability — solves the distribution problem: institutional yield, permissionless access. The 25% revenue buyback creates a direct earnings-to-token link that most DeFi protocols lack entirely.

At ~6.6× FDV/ARR it is the cheapest protocol in the set on a revenue basis. The risk is that this cheap multiple reflects the Orthogonal shadow — institutional allocators who lost capital in 2022 have long memories, and a second default event would be existential.

Bull: $100M ARR by end-2026 with SYRUP buybacks at scale — at a 15–20× multiple that implies a $1.5–2.0B FDV, roughly 10× from current levels.

Bear: Credit contraction or default destroys AUM faster than it was built. Management's $100M ARR target requires 4× revenue growth in 12 months from a base that took years to reach.

Thesis and Risks
Centrifuge is the infrastructure play in the coverage set — it does not compete for AUM directly but provides the compliance, structuring, and distribution rails for TradFi asset managers to issue on-chain. The Janus Henderson CLO is the most significant institutional validation in the space: a top-20 global AM using a blockchain protocol as primary distribution infrastructure.

At 21× TVL/FDV and ~5× FDV/ARR, CFG is statistically the cheapest protocol in the set. The question is whether the token accrues value in proportion to TVL growth — historically it has not.

Bull: Becomes the DTCC of on-chain assets — a utility layer whose revenue scales with total tokenized AUM. If global RWA TVL reaches $100B by end-2026 as Centrifuge Labs projects, even a 1% market share implies $1B+ AUM on CFG rails.

Bear: $15M ARR on $1.6B TVL is a thin take-rate. Competing infrastructure (Securitize, Tokenized Asset Coalition) can replicate rails without a native token, potentially removing the economic rationale for CFG entirely.
